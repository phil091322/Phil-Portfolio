Case Studies

Project Overview

Name of the project: HRS INVENTORY SYSTEM

Description and objectives: The system provides a centralized platform for monitoring inventory, automating stock updates, and generating reports. It helps businesses maintain real-time visibility into their assets, reducing manual errors and improving overall efficiency.

Accurate Inventory Tracking – Prevent stock discrepancies by maintaining real-time updates. Optimized Resource Management – Ensure proper allocation of materials and supplies. Automated Stock Replenishment – Set reorder levels to avoid shortages or overstocking. Improved Decision-Making – Generate insightful reports for better forecasting and planning. Enhanced Security & Access Control – Restrict unauthorized access to sensitive inventory data.

Your role: Proccess Maker, Software Tester, Presentor of the System

Process Maker: designed and structured the workflow of the system, ensuring that inventory management operations run smoothly. Your responsibilities include: Defining system processes for stock monitoring, replenishment, and reporting. Mapping out inventory workflows to improve efficiency and reduce manual errors. Integrating automation features to streamline inventory tracking and stock control. Collaborating with stakeholders to tailor processes to business requirements.

Software Tester: played a crucial role in ensuring system stability and functionality. Your responsibilities include: Conducting rigorous functional and usability testing to identify bugs or inconsistencies. Validating system performance under different use cases and stress conditions. Collaborating with developers to resolve issues and enhance reliability. Ensuring security measures are in place to protect inventory data from unauthorized access.

Presenter: effectively communicated the system’s capabilities and benefits to stakeholders, ensuring clear understanding and adoption. Your responsibilities include: Preparing and delivering detailed presentations on system functionality and advantages. Demonstrating key features, guiding users through system interactions. Addressing questions and concerns from clients, team members, or decision-makers. Creating documentation or training materials to support system onboarding.

Problem Statement

Challenge faced:

Inventory Records – Discrepancies between recorded and actual stock levels lead to stockouts or overstocking, affecting business operations.

No System Available: The school does not have any monitoring system with our HRS course Inventory, we decide to initiate some survey with the one who manage the inventory and agreed, we took the initative to introduce the system and provide the solution offer and the scope of the system. The case study was approved so and we started developing the System

Concerns – Unauthorized access or data breaches can compromise sensitive inventory information.

Limitations – As School grow, the system may struggle to handle increased inventory volumes efficiently.

Mismanagement – Poor demand forecasting leads to either excess inventory or shortages, impacting profitability.

Reason for initiation:

The project was initiated due to inefficiencies in inventory management, leading to frequent stock discrepancies and operational delays.

School faced challenges in tracking stock levels accurately, resulting in overstocking or shortages that affected profitability.

Manual inventory processes were prone to human errors, requiring a system that could automate stock updates and improve accuracy.

There was a need for a centralized platform to streamline inventory tracking, optimize resource allocation, and enhance decision-making.

Security concerns regarding unauthorized access to inventory data prompted the development of a system with robust access controls.

Scalability issues in existing inventory solutions made it difficult for businesses to expand operations efficiently.

To address these challenges, the project was initiated to develop an inventory system that integrates automation, security, and real-time tracking.

Process & Approach

Methodology:

Waterfall Framework – The project followed a linear development approach, ensuring each phase was completed before moving to the next.

Design Thinking – A user-centric approach was applied to create an intuitive inventory management experience.

Test-Driven Development (TDD) – Rigorous testing was conducted at the end of each development phase to ensure system stability.

Tools & technologies:

Programming Languages – The system was built using Visual Studio, Visual basic & C#

Database Management – MySQL was used to store and manage inventory records securely.

UI/UX Design – Photoshop was used for prototyping and designing an intuitive user interface.

Collaboration efforts: [Teamwork details]

Faculty Consultation – Meetings were conducted with professors to align the project with academic standards and institutional needs.

Survey & Feedback – Surveys were distributed to faculty members to gather insights on inventory challenges and workflow improvements.

Team Collaboration – Group discussions among project members ensured a coordinated approach in research, development, and testing.

Professor Guidance – Faculty advisors provided mentorship, ensuring the project met educational and technical expectations.

Documentation & Presentation – Reports and presentations were prepared for academic evaluation and project defense.
